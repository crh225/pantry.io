# User Personas for Pantry.io

## Persona 1: Busy Professional - Sarah

**Demographics:**
- Age: 32
- Occupation: Marketing Manager
- Location: Urban apartment
- Tech Savvy: High

**Goals:**
- Reduce food waste by using ingredients before expiration
- Save time on meal planning and grocery shopping
- Use up ingredients already in pantry
- Avoid multiple grocery trips per week

**Pain Points:**
- Often forgets what's in the pantry and buys duplicates
- Struggles to think of recipes with available ingredients
- Limited time for meal planning during busy work week
- Food spoils before she can use it
- Overwhelmed by too many recipe options online

**Expectations:**
- Fast, intuitive app that works on mobile and desktop
- Quick pantry inventory management (add/remove items in seconds)
- Instant recipe suggestions without browsing multiple sites
- Shopping lists that sync with what she already has
- Clean, simple interface without ads or clutter
- Reliable recipe database with accurate information

**How Pantry.io Helps:**
- Quick pantry inventory tracking with location tags (pantry/fridge/freezer)
- Instant recipe suggestions based on current inventory
- Shopping list generation showing only missing ingredients
- Match percentage shows best recipes to minimize shopping
- Minimal white UI keeps focus on recipes and ingredients

## Persona 2: Budget-Conscious Student - Mike

**Demographics:**
- Age: 22
- Occupation: College Student
- Location: Shared apartment with 2 roommates
- Tech Savvy: High

**Goals:**
- Cook meals on a tight budget ($40-50/week for groceries)
- Minimize food waste to maximize dollar value
- Find simple recipes with 5-7 ingredients maximum
- Make the most of what's already in the shared kitchen
- Learn basic cooking skills

**Pain Points:**
- Limited budget means every purchase counts
- Shared kitchen with roommates makes inventory confusing
- Not confident in cooking skills, often defaults to takeout
- Recipe websites have too many ads and long stories
- Can't afford to buy specialty ingredients
- Grocery stores are expensive near campus

**Expectations:**
- Free app with no subscription required
- Simple recipes that don't require advanced techniques
- Clear ingredient lists with measurements
- Works on phone (doesn't have laptop)
- No credit card or account creation required
- Fast loading without heavy graphics

**How Pantry.io Helps:**
- Completely free recipe database (TheMealDB API)
- Identifies recipes requiring minimal additional purchases
- Shows match percentage to maximize existing ingredients
- Step-by-step instructions with photos for cooking guidance
- No account required, works immediately
- Mobile-optimized responsive design

## Persona 3: Family Meal Planner - Jennifer

**Demographics:**
- Age: 38
- Occupation: Stay-at-home parent
- Location: Suburban home
- Family Size: 4 (2 adults, 2 kids ages 7 and 10)
- Tech Savvy: Medium

**Goals:**
- Plan diverse meals for family of 4 throughout the week
- Reduce monthly grocery costs (currently $800/month)
- Cook variety of meals to keep family interested
- Keep accurate inventory of pantry, fridge, and freezer
- Reduce food waste (currently throws away ~$50/week)
- Find kid-friendly recipes that adults enjoy too

**Pain Points:**
- Hard to remember everything stored across locations
- Kids are picky eaters with different preferences
- Needs to plan 21 meals per week (breakfast/lunch/dinner)
- Wants variety but often makes same 10 recipes on repeat
- Grocery shopping takes 2+ hours without a good list
- Food gets lost in back of pantry/freezer and expires

**Expectations:**
- Easy-to-use interface (not too technical)
- Ability to track items in different locations
- Recipe variety across different cuisines
- Clear photos of finished dishes
- Shopping list that consolidates all missing items
- Works on tablet in kitchen while cooking
- Saves time compared to manual meal planning

**How Pantry.io Helps:**
- Comprehensive pantry management across pantry/fridge/freezer locations
- Variety of recipe options searchable by cuisine type
- Easy-to-follow recipe instructions with large photos
- Shopping list consolidation shows all missing ingredients in one place
- Match percentage helps use up existing ingredients first
- Mobile and tablet responsive design for kitchen use
- Local storage keeps inventory between sessions

## Common Themes Across Personas

**Shared Goals:**
- Reduce food waste
- Save money on groceries
- Simplify meal planning process
- Use ingredients before expiration

**Shared Pain Points:**
- Difficulty remembering what's in storage
- Buying duplicate items unnecessarily
- Limited time/skills for meal planning
- Recipe overwhelm from too many options

**Shared Expectations:**
- Fast, simple interface
- Mobile-friendly design
- Free to use
- Immediate value without setup
- Accurate recipe information
- Visual recipe presentation

**How Pantry.io Addresses All:**
- Single-purpose app focused on pantry-based meal planning
- Free recipe database requires no API keys
- Minimal white design eliminates distractions
- Match percentage algorithm prioritizes existing ingredients
- Shopping list generation reduces waste and costs
- Responsive design works on all devices
- Local storage requires no account creation
